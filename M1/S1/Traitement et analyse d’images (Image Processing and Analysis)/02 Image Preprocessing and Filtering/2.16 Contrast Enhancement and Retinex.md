

This section deals with defining contrast mathematically and enhancing it using spatial derivatives and physics-based illumination models.

## 1. Defining Contrast

Contrast is the difference in luminance that makes an object distinguishable.
*   **Global Contrast (RMS):** Standard deviation of pixel intensities.
    $$ C = \sqrt{\frac{1}{M \times N} \sum \sum (I(i,j) - \bar{I})^2} $$
*   **Range Contrast:**
    $$ C = \frac{X_{max} - X_{min}}{X_{max} + X_{min}} $$

## 2. Edge Enhancement (Laplacian)

To make an image look "sharper," we accentuate the edges. This is based on adding the **second derivative** (Laplacian) to the original image.

*   **The Logic:**
    1.  The Laplacian ($\nabla^2 f$) is zero in flat areas.
    2.  It creates a positive spike on one side of an edge and a negative spike on the other.
    3.  Adding this to the original image boosts the transitions.
*   **Formula:** $g(x,y) = f(x,y) + c(\nabla^2 f(x,y))$
    *   If $c > 0$: Sharpening.
    *   Note: The sign depends on the specific Laplacian mask used (whether the center is positive or negative).

## 3. Retinex Theory (Illumination Correction)

Retinex (Retina + Cortex) theory explains how the human eye perceives color as constant regardless of lighting.

### The Model
An observed image $I(x,y)$ is the product of:
1.  **Reflectance $R(x,y)$:** The true color/texture of the object (What we want).
2.  **Illumination $L(x,y)$:** The light falling on the scene (What we want to remove).

$$ I(x,y) = R(x,y) \times L(x,y) $$

### The Algorithm (SSR - Single Scale Retinex)
Since multiplication is hard to separate, we move to the **Logarithmic Domain** to turn multiplication into addition/subtraction.

1.  **Logarithm:** $\log(I) = \log(R) + \log(L)$
2.  **Assumption:** Illumination $L$ changes slowly (low frequency), while Reflectance $R$ changes quickly (edges, texture).
3.  **Estimation:** We can estimate $L$ by heavily blurring the image $I$ (using a Gaussian $G$).
    $$ \text{Estimated } \log(L) \approx \log(I * G) $$
4.  **Subtraction:**
    $$ \log(R) = \log(I) - \log(I * G) $$
5.  **Output:** The result is an image with the lighting cast removed, showing details in shadows.

**Multi-Scale Retinex (MSR):** The slides mention MSR (Slide 198), which simply averages the result of SSR performed with different Gaussian sizes (small, medium, large) to capture both fine details and global lighting.
