# 3.1. A Detailed History of Neural Networks

The history of Deep Learning is not a straight line; it is characterized by massive breakthroughs followed by "AI Winters" where funding and interest dried up.

- **1943 - 1960: Biological Inspiration**
  - McCulloch & Pitts modeled the first artificial neuron.
  - Frank Rosenblatt invented the **Perceptron**, the earliest form of a neural network.
- **1980 - 1998: The First Golden Age (Learning & Vision)**
  - **1986 (The Turning Point):** Geoffrey Hinton popularized **Backpropagation**, making it possible to train multi-layer networks.
  - Yann LeCun invented CNNs, allowing computers to recognize handwritten digits (used by banks to read checks).
  - **1990:** Yoshua Bengio combined ANNs with probabilistic sequence models. His team improved machine translation using early "attention" mechanisms (laying the groundwork for modern LLMs).
- **The "Dark Ages" (up to 2006)**
  - Neural networks were abandoned. They required too much computation and were notoriously difficult to train.
  - Statistical methods like **SVM (Support Vector Machines)**, **DT (Decision Trees)**, and **RF (Random Forests)** dominated the field because they were less complex and mathematically guaranteed to find optimal solutions.
- **2012: The Grand Shift (AlexNet & ImageNet)**
  - The **ImageNet Challenge (ILSVRC)** asked participants to classify 1 million annotated images into 1,000 distinct classes.
  - Geoffrey Hinton's team achieved a crushing victory, cutting the error rate in half compared to traditional methods.
  - **Hinton's Genius Idea:** He repurposed **GPUs (Graphics Processing Units)**, originally designed for video games, to perform the massive parallel matrix multiplications required by neural networks.
- **2014: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)**
  - Ian Goodfellow invented GANs, allowing networks to "create" highly realistic fake images by pitting two networks against each other.
- **2016: Media Explosion**
  - DeepMind's AlphaGo defeated the human world champion at the game of Go, a game with a complexity far exceeding Chess.
- **2017 - Present: The Generative AI Era**
  - **2017:** Google invents the **Transformer** architecture (the "Attention is All You Need" paper).
  - **2018:** LeCun, Hinton, and Bengio win the **Turing Award** ($1M prize, considered the Nobel Prize of Computing).
  - **2020+:** The rise of Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-3, ChatGPT, Gemini, and LLaMA, utilizing billions of parameters.
