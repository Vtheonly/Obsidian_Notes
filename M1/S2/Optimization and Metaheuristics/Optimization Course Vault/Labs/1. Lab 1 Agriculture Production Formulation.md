> [!info] Essential Background Knowledge
> **Linear Programming (LP) Formulation** involves translating a real-world problem into a mathematical model. Every LP model requires three main components:
>
> 1. **Decision Variables:** What are we trying to determine?
> 2. **Objective Function:** What is the goal (maximize profit, minimize cost)?
> 3. **Constraints:** What are the physical, financial, or logical limits?
>
> The **Canonical Form** for a Maximization problem requires all constraints to be written as "Less than or equal to" ($\le$) and all variables to be non-negative.

### 1. Problem Statement

_(From Images 1 & 2)_
An agriculturist wants to optimize the production of two crops: potatoes and onions.

- The yield is **4 kg/m² for potatoes** and **5 kg/m² for onions**.
- To increase production, they use two types of fertilizers: A and B.
- **Available Stock:** 8 Liters of A, 7 Liters of B.

**Usage rates per square meter:**

- **Fertilizer A:** Requires 2 L/m² for potatoes and 1 L/m² for onions.
- **Fertilizer B:** Requires 1 L/m² for potatoes and 2 L/m² for onions.
- **Parasite Control:** The farmer has 3 Liters of an anti-parasite treatment. This is used _only_ for onions at a rate of 1 L/m².

**Goal:** Write the mathematical problem in its canonical form.

---

### 2. Step-by-Step Resolution

#### Step 1: Define the Decision Variables

> [!warning] Common Pitfall
> Students often define variables as "Fertilizer A" or "Potatoes". Variables must be quantifiable! Define _what exactly_ you are measuring. Here, we control the surface area.

- Let $x_1$ = the surface area planted with potatoes (in m²).
- Let $x_2$ = the surface area planted with onions (in m²).

#### Step 2: Formulate the Objective Function

The goal is to maximize total production weight.

- Potatoes yield 4 kg per m², so total potato yield is $4x_1$.
- Onions yield 5 kg per m², so total onion yield is $5x_2$.

$$Max(Z) = 4x_1 + 5x_2$$

#### Step 3: Formulate the Constraints

We build constraints based on the limited resources.

**1. Fertilizer A Limit:**

- Potatoes use 2 L/m² ($2x_1$), Onions use 1 L/m² ($1x_2$). Limit: 8 L.
- Constraint: $2x_1 + x_2 \le 8$

**2. Fertilizer B Limit:**

- Potatoes use 1 L/m² ($1x_1$), Onions use 2 L/m² ($2x_2$). Limit: 7 L.
- Constraint: $x_1 + 2x_2 \le 7$

**3. Anti-Parasite Limit:**

- Only used for onions at 1 L/m² ($1x_2$). Potatoes use 0 L. Limit: 3 L.
- Constraint: $x_2 \le 3$

**4. Non-negativity Constraints:**

- You cannot plant a negative area.
- Constraint: $x_1 \ge 0, x_2 \ge 0$

---

### 3. Final Canonical Model

> [!success] The Complete Model
> $$Max(Z) = 4x_1 + 5x_2$$
> **Subject to (s.t.):**
> $$2x_1 + x_2 \le 8$$
> $$x_1 + 2x_2 \le 7$$
> $$x_2 \le 3$$
> $$x_1, x_2 \ge 0$$
