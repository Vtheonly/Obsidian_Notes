> [!info] Background Knowledge: Diet Problems
> In diet or mixture problems, the objective is usually to **minimize cost** while meeting **minimum nutritional requirements**. Because these are minimum thresholds, the constraints are naturally structured as "Greater than or equal to" ($\ge$).

### 1. Problem Statement

_(From Images 3, 4, 18)_
A hospital patient must consume a mix of two types of food (Aliment 1 and Aliment 2) to meet daily nutritional limits.

- Minimum Vitamin C required: **3600 mg**
- Minimum Protein required: **400 g**

**Food Nutritional Values & Costs:**

| Element | Aliment 1 ($x_1$) | Aliment 2 ($x_2$) | Requirements (Constraints) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Vit C (mg)** | 20 | 60 | $\ge 3600$ |
| **Protein (g)** | 2 | 8 | $\ge 400$ |
| **Cost (DA)** | 30 | 100 | **Minimize (Z)** |

**Goal:** Formulate the model and solve it graphically.

---

### 2. General Mathematical Model

- **Variables:** $x_1$ = qty of Aliment 1, $x_2$ = qty of Aliment 2.
- **Objective:** $Min(Z) = 30x_1 + 100x_2$
- **Constraints:**
  - $20x_1 + 60x_2 \ge 3600$ (Vitamin C)
  - $2x_1 + 8x_2 \ge 400$ (Protein)
  - $x_1, x_2 \ge 0$

---

### 3. Graphical Resolution Step by Step

To plot constraints, calculate their intercepts on the $x_1$ and $x_2$ axes.

**Line 1 (Vitamin C):** $20x_1 + 60x_2 = 3600$

- Set $x_1 = 0 \implies 60x_2 = 3600 \implies x_2 = 60 \rightarrow Point: (0, 60)$
- Set $x_2 = 0 \implies 20x_1 = 3600 \implies x_1 = 180 \rightarrow Point: (180, 0)$

**Line 2 (Protein):** $2x_1 + 8x_2 = 400$

- Set $x_1 = 0 \implies 8x_2 = 400 \implies x_2 = 50 \rightarrow Point: (0, 50)$
- Set $x_2 = 0 \implies 2x_1 = 400 \implies x_1 = 200 \rightarrow Point: (200, 0)$

**Finding the Intersection of Line 1 and Line 2:**

1. $20x_1 + 60x_2 = 3600 \implies x_1 + 3x_2 = 180 \implies x_1 = 180 - 3x_2$
2. Substitute into Line 2: $2(180 - 3x_2) + 8x_2 = 400 \implies 360 - 6x_2 + 8x_2 = 400 \implies 2x_2 = 40 \implies x_2 = 20$
3. $x_1 = 180 - 3(20) = 120$
   **Intersection Point:** $(120, 20)$

#### Evaluating Vertices

Because constraints are $\ge$, the feasible region is _unbounded_ (open to the top right). The boundary vertices are:

- $A (0, 60): Z = 30(0) + 100(60) = 6000$ DA
- **$B (120, 20): Z = 30(120) + 100(20) = 3600 + 2000 = 5600$ DA $\leftarrow$ OPTIMAL**
- $C (200, 0): Z = 30(200) + 100(0) = 6000$ DA

> [!success] Conclusion
> The optimal diet consists of **120 units of Aliment 1** and **20 units of Aliment 2**, yielding a minimum cost of **5600 DA**.
