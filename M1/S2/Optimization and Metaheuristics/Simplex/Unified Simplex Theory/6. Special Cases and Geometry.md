
# 6. Special Cases and Geometry

Sometimes things go wrong. Here is how to identify them in the Tableau.

## **6.1. Degeneracy**
*   **Symptom:** In the Ratio Test (Step 3), there is a **tie** for the minimum ratio.
*   **Meaning:** Geometrically, the Simplex is pivoting but staying on the same vertex (or cycling between equivalent vertices).
*   **Solution:** Pick either one. Ideally, pick the one highest up in the table to prevent cycling (Bland's Rule).

## **6.2. Unbounded Solution**
*   **Symptom:** When looking for the Leaving Variable (Pivot Row), **all** entries in the Pivot Column are $\le 0$.
*   **Result:** You cannot calculate a valid ratio (cannot divide by negative or zero).
*   **Meaning:** The feasible region goes on forever in the direction of profit. Profit is infinite. Stop the calculation.

## **6.3. Multiple Optimal Solutions**
*   **Symptom:** You reach the optimal table (all $C_j - Z_j \le 0$), BUT one of the **Non-Basic** variables has a value of **0** in the $C_j - Z_j$ row.
*   **Meaning:** You can bring that variable into the basis without changing the $Z$ value. You are moving along an edge between two optimal vertices. The line of the objective function is parallel to a constraint line.

## **6.4. Infeasible Solution**
*   **Symptom:** You reach the "Optimal" table (stop condition met), but an **Artificial Variable ($a_i$)** is still in the Basis (it has a non-zero value).
*   **Meaning:** There is no solution that satisfies all constraints simultaneously.

Both tables tell you to pick **$x_2$** as the entering variable. Both are correct. **Stick to Method A** if you want to understand the provided "Intro" files, as it is more descriptive.
