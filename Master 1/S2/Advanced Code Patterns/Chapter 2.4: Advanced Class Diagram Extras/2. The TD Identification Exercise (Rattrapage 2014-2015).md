
# 2. The TD Identification Exercise (Rattrapage 2014-2015)

In the Rattrapage exam (PDF 18), Exercice 1 (2.5 pts), the professor gives you 5 textual scenarios and asks you to explicitly identify the relationship: **Généralisation (héritage), Agrégation, Composition, or Association.** 

This tests your intuitive understanding of UML semantics. Here is the exact breakdown and reasoning for each:

> [!WARNING] The Golden Rule for this Exercise
> If you can say "is a" (est un), it's Inheritance.
> If you can say "has a" (a un) and destroying the whole destroys the part, it's Composition.
> If you can say "has a" but the part survives, it's Aggregation.
> If none of the above perfectly fit, it's an Association.

### 1. "Un pays a une capitale." (A country has a capital)
* **Correct Answer:** **Association** (specifically, a 1-to-1 association).
* **Reasoning:** A capital is a City (`Ville`). A country does not "physically contain the concept of a city in a whole-part destruction way" in standard modeling. It is simply a designated role. `Pays "1" -- "1" Ville : a pour capitale`.

### 2. "Une salle de cours et des tables." (A classroom and tables)
* **Correct Answer:** **Agrégation** (Aggregation).
* **Reasoning:** The classroom contains tables (Whole-Part). However, if you demolish the classroom, you can take the tables out and put them in another room. The tables survive the destruction of the room. Therefore, it is a weak whole-part relationship (Empty Diamond on Salle).

### 3. "Les fichiers contiennent des enregistrements." (Files contain records)
* **Correct Answer:** **Composition**.
* **Reasoning:** A record (ligne de base de données / data entry) exists purely *inside* that specific file. If you delete the file, the records inside it are permanently destroyed. Their lifecycles are strictly bound. (Filled Black Diamond on Fichier).

### 4. "Une personne utilise un langage de programmation dans un projet."
* **Correct Answer:** **Association** (Specifically, an **Association Ternaire / N-ary**).
* **Reasoning:** It links three distinct concepts: `Personne`, `Langage`, and `Projet` simultaneously. You cannot break this into pairs without losing the context of *who* used *what* on *which* project.

### 5. "Les modems et les claviers sont des périphériques d'entrées/sorties."
* **Correct Answer:** **Généralisation (Héritage)**.
* **Reasoning:** A modem *IS A* peripheral. A keyboard *IS A* peripheral. They inherit the properties of the `Périphérique` superclass.
