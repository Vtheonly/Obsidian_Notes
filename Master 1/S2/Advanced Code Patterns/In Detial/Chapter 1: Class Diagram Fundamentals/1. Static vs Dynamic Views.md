
# 1. Static vs Dynamic Views

To score perfectly on the theoretical questions of your MCA exams, you must understand the dichotomy of UML diagrams. A recurring exam question asks: *"What is the difference between a static (logic) view and a dynamic view? Cite two diagrams for each."*

### The Core Concept
UML (Unified Modeling Language) allows us to build models of software systems from multiple perspectives. We divide these perspectives into two main categories:

1. **Static View (Structural)**
   * **Definition:** Represents the structure of the model **without taking time or evolution into account**. It shows the components of the system and how they are rigidly connected.
   * **Think of it as:** A blueprint of a building or a snapshot photograph.
   * **Diagrams included:** 
     * **Class Diagram** (Diagramme de classes) - The most important one!
     * **Object Diagram** (Diagramme d'objets)
     * **Package Diagram** (Diagramme de paquetages)

2. **Dynamic View (Behavioral)**
   * **Definition:** Represents the **changes and behaviors** that occur in the system **over time**. It shows how objects interact, the flow of control, and how data moves.
   * **Think of it as:** A movie, or the electrical/plumbing flow in a building over time.
   * **Diagrams included:**
     * **Sequence Diagram** (Diagramme de séquences)
     * **Activity Diagram** (Diagramme d'activités)
     * State Machine Diagram (Diagramme d'états-transitions)

### 💡 Exam Tip & Common Pitfall
> **Exam Trick:** In textual analysis exercises (like the *RQV vehicle theft* or the *Telemedicine* exams), professors will ask you to *"Elaborer la vue logique"* (Elaborate the logical view). **Logical View is synonymous with Static View**, which means they are asking for a **Class Diagram**. Do not draw a Sequence Diagram when asked for the "Logical View"!
