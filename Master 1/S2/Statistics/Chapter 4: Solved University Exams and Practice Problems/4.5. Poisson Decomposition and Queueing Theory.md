# 4.5. Poisson Decomposition and Queueing Theory

### 1. Mixed Vehicle Traffic (Decomposition Solver)
* **Problem Statement:** The flow of vehicles on a road is modeled as a Poisson process with an average rate of 1 vehicle every 10 seconds. On average, $10\%$ of the vehicles are trucks and $90\%$ are cars.
  1. Determine the Poisson parameters for total vehicles ($\lambda$), trucks ($\lambda_1$), and cars ($\lambda_2$) per minute.
  2. Compute the probability of at least one truck passing in a given minute.
  3. Compute the probability of at most 2 cars passing in a given minute.
  4. Given that 10 trucks passed during a 5-minute interval, compute the expected total number of vehicles that passed during this interval.

#### Determining the Parameters per Minute
The average rate is 1 vehicle every 10 seconds, which corresponds to 6 vehicles per minute:
$$\lambda = 6 \text{ vehicles/minute}$$

Using the properties of Poisson decomposition, we find the parameters for trucks and cars:
$$\lambda_1 = 10\% \times \lambda = 0.10 \times 6 = 0.6 \text{ trucks/minute}$$
$$\lambda_2 = 90\% \times \lambda = 0.90 \times 6 = 5.4 \text{ cars/minute}$$

#### Probability of at least one Truck in 1 Minute
We can calculate this using the complement probability:
$$P(N_1(1) \ge 1) = 1 - P(N_1(1) = 0) = 1 - \frac{e^{-0.6} (0.6)^0}{0!} = 1 - e^{-0.6} \approx 1 - 0.54881 = 0.45119$$

#### Probability of at most 2 Cars in 1 Minute
$$P(N_2(1) \le 2) = P(N_2(1) = 0) + P(N_2(1) = 1) + P(N_2(1) = 2) = e^{-5.4} + (5.4)e^{-5.4} + \frac{(5.4)^2}{2}e^{-5.4}$$
$$P(N_2(1) \le 2) = e^{-5.4} \left( 1 + 5.4 + 14.58 \right) = 20.98 e^{-5.4} \approx 20.98 \times 0.004517 = 0.09476$$

#### Expected Total Vehicles given 10 Trucks in 5 Minutes
Since the decomposed processes for trucks ($N_1(t)$) and cars ($N_2(t)$) are independent, the number of cars passing during the 5-minute interval is independent of the number of trucks that passed.

The expected number of cars in a 5-minute interval is:
$$E(N_2(5)) = 5 \times \lambda_2 = 5 \times 5.4 = 27 \text{ cars}$$

Given that exactly 10 trucks passed, the expected total number of vehicles is:
$$E(\text{Total}) = \text{Trucks} + E(\text{Cars}) = 10 + 27 = 37 \text{ vehicles}$$

### 2. M/M/1 Queue and Little's Formula
* **Problem Statement:** An M/M/1 queue has an average of 1 customer arriving every 12 minutes, and an average service time of 8 minutes.
  1. Determine the arrival rate ($\lambda$) and service rate ($\mu$) per hour.
  2. Compute the average number of customers in the system ($L$), average number of customers in the queue ($L_q$), average time spent in the system ($W$), and average waiting time in the queue ($W_q$).
  3. If the arrival rate increases by $20\%$, compute the new metrics $L^*$ and $W^*$, and find their percentage increases.

#### Determining the Rates per Hour
* **Arrival Rate ($\lambda$):**
  $$\lambda = \frac{1 \text{ customer}}{12 \text{ minutes}} = \frac{60 \text{ minutes/hour}}{12 \text{ minutes}} = 5 \text{ customers/hour}$$
* **Service Rate ($\mu$):**
  $$\mu = \frac{1 \text{ customer}}{8 \text{ minutes}} = \frac{60 \text{ minutes/hour}}{8 \text{ minutes}} = 7.5 \text{ customers/hour}$$

#### Computing System Metrics
* **Average Number of Customers in the System ($L$):**
  $$L = \frac{\lambda}{\mu - \lambda} = \frac{5}{7.5 - 5} = \frac{5}{2.5} = 2 \text{ customers}$$
* **Average Number of Customers in the Queue ($L_q$):**
  $$L_q = \frac{\lambda^2}{\mu(\mu - \lambda)} = \frac{5^2}{7.5(7.5 - 5)} = \frac{25}{7.5 \times 2.5} = \frac{25}{18.75} = \frac{4}{3} \approx 1.33 \text{ customers}$$
* **Average Time Spent in the System ($W$):**
  $$W = \frac{1}{\mu - \lambda} = \frac{1}{7.5 - 5} = \frac{1}{2.5} = 0.4 \text{ hours} = 24 \text{ minutes}$$
* **Average Waiting Time in the Queue ($W_q$):**
  $$W_q = \frac{\lambda}{\mu(\mu - \lambda)} = \frac{5}{7.5(7.5 - 5)} = \frac{5}{18.75} = \frac{4}{15} \text{ hours} = 16 \text{ minutes}$$

#### Analyzing a $20\%$ Increase in Arrival Rate
The new arrival rate is:
$$\lambda^* = 1.20 \times \lambda = 1.20 \times 5 = 6 \text{ customers/hour}$$

The service rate remains unchanged ($\mu = 7.5$):
* **New Average Number of Customers ($L^*$):**
  $$L^* = \frac{\lambda^*}{\mu - \lambda^*} = \frac{6}{7.5 - 6} = \frac{6}{1.5} = 4 \text{ customers}$$
  This is a $100\%$ increase in the average number of customers:
  $$\text{Increase in } L = \frac{4 - 2}{2} \times 100\% = 100\%$$
* **New Average Time in the System ($W^*$):**
  $$W^* = \frac{1}{\mu - \lambda^*} = \frac{1}{7.5 - 6} = \frac{1}{1.5} = \frac{2}{3} \text{ hours} = 40 \text{ minutes}$$
  This is a $66.67\%$ increase in the average time spent in the system:
  $$\text{Increase in } W = \frac{40 - 24}{24} \times 100\% = 66.67\%$$
