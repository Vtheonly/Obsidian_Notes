### 1. Problem Statement

_(From Image 23)_
A restaurateur offers two types of seafood plates:

- **Plate 1 (80 DA):** 5 sardines, 2 merlans, 1 rouge.
- **Plate 2 (120 DA):** 3 sardines, 3 merlans, 3 rouges.

**Kitchen Inventory Limits:** 30 Sardines, 24 Merlans, 18 Rouges.
**Goal:** Maximize revenue.

### 2. Step-by-step Formulation

- **Decision Variables:**
  - $x_1$ = Number of Plate 1 prepared.
  - $x_2$ = Number of Plate 2 prepared.
- **Objective Function:** $Max(Z) = 80x_1 + 120x_2$
- **Constraints:**
  - Sardines: $5x_1 + 3x_2 \le 30$
  - Merlans: $2x_1 + 3x_2 \le 24$
  - Rouges: $1x_1 + 3x_2 \le 18$
  - Positivity: $x_1, x_2 \ge 0$
