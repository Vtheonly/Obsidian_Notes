> [!info] Concept: Saturation of Constraints
> A constraint is **saturated** (or binding) if plugging the optimal solution into it results in an exact equality. This means the resource is 100% utilized (or a minimum requirement is exactly met with zero surplus). If it's not equal, it's non-saturated, meaning there is "slack" or "surplus".

### 1. Analysis of the Hospital Diet Problem

_(From Image 9)_
Let's verify the constraint saturation for the optimal solution found in Lab 2: **$x_1 = 120, x_2 = 20$**.

**1. Verification of Vitamin C Constraint:**

- Equation: $20x_1 + 60x_2 \ge 3600$
- Substitute: $20(120) + 60(20)$
- Calculation: $2400 + 1200 = 3600$
- Result: $3600 = 3600 \implies$ **Saturated Constraint.**

**2. Verification of Protein Constraint:**

- Equation: $2x_1 + 8x_2 \ge 400$
- Substitute: $2(120) + 8(20)$
- Calculation: $240 + 160 = 400$
- Result: $400 = 400 \implies$ **Saturated Constraint.**

> [!tip] Interpretation
> Because both constraints are saturated, the chosen diet ($x_1=120, x_2=20$) meets the nutritional needs _exactly_ without providing any excess vitamins or protein. From a financial optimization perspective, this is perfect because excess nutrition represents wasted money!
