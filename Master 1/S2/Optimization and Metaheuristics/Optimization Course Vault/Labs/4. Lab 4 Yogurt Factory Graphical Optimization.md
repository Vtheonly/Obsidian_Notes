> [!info] Convex Polygon Theory
> _(From Image 24)_
> If the region of feasible solutions is a **closed convex set** (a polygon bounded on all sides), the optimal solution is mathematically guaranteed to exist at one of its vertices.

### 1. Problem Statement

_(From Images 14, 15, 17, 24)_
A manufacturer produces two yogurts: Type A (Strawberry) and Type B (Plain/Sweetened).
**Resources:** 800 kg Strawberries, 700 kg Milk, 300 kg Sugar.

| Material         | Yogurt A ($x_1$) | Yogurt B ($x_2$) | Constraints  |
| :--------------- | :--------------- | :--------------- | :----------- |
| **Strawberries** | 2 kg             | 1 kg             | $\le 800$    |
| **Milk**         | 1 kg             | 2 kg             | $\le 700$    |
| **Sugar**        | 0 kg             | 1 kg             | $\le 300$    |
| **Profit**       | 4 DA/unit        | 5 DA/unit        | **Maximize** |

### 2. Model Formulation

- **Objective:** $Max(Z) = 4x_1 + 5x_2$
- **Constraints:**
  - $2x_1 + x_2 \le 800$
  - $x_1 + 2x_2 \le 700$
  - $x_2 \le 300$
  - $x_1, x_2 \ge 0$

### 3. Graphical Resolution

**Finding Intercepts:**

- **L1 (Strawberries):** $(0, 800)$ and $(400, 0)$
- **L2 (Milk):** $(0, 350)$ and $(700, 0)$
- **L3 (Sugar):** Horizontal line at $y = 300$

**Finding Polygon Vertices (Intersections):**

- **A:** Origin $(0,0)$
- **B:** L3 and y-axis $\rightarrow (0, 300)$
- **C:** Intersection L3 ($x_2=300$) and L2 ($x_1 + 2x_2 = 700$). $x_1 + 2(300) = 700 \implies x_1 = 100$. $\rightarrow (100, 300)$
- **D:** Intersection L1 and L2.
  - $x_1 = 700 - 2x_2$
  - $2(700 - 2x_2) + x_2 = 800 \implies 1400 - 3x_2 = 800 \implies 3x_2 = 600 \implies x_2 = 200$.
  - $x_1 = 700 - 2(200) = 300$. $\rightarrow (300, 200)$
- **E:** L1 and x-axis $\rightarrow (400, 0)$

**Evaluating Z at Vertices:**

- $A: Z = 0$
- $B: Z = 4(0) + 5(300) = 1500$
- $C: Z = 4(100) + 5(300) = 1900$
- **$D: Z = 4(300) + 5(200) = 2200$ $\leftarrow$ Maximum Profit**
- $E: Z = 4(400) + 5(0) = 1600$

> [!success] Final Result
> Produce 300 units of Yogurt A and 200 units of Yogurt B to earn 2200 DA.
