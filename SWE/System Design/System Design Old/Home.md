# System Design Vault

## Welcome

This vault contains a comprehensive collection of system design knowledge, covering the full spectrum from hardware foundations to high-performance distributed systems. Every topic is explained in depth with rigorous detail, practical examples, and cross-references to related concepts. The material is organized into twelve logically sequenced chapters, each building on the knowledge established in the preceding sections.

Whether you are preparing for system design interviews, studying distributed systems architecture, or seeking to deepen your understanding of how large-scale systems operate, this vault provides the depth and breadth required for mastery.

## How to Navigate

Start with the [[Map of Content]] for a complete overview of every chapter and topic. Each chapter also has its own index page that links to all topics within that chapter. Every individual note includes a "Related Topics" section with Obsidian wikilinks to connect you to adjacent concepts.

## Study Path

The recommended study sequence follows the chapter order:

1. Begin with hardware and architecture fundamentals to understand the physical constraints that shape all software decisions
2. Progress through system architecture and scaling strategies to learn how single servers evolve into distributed systems
3. Study networking and communication protocols to understand how distributed components communicate
4. Explore API design patterns to learn how systems expose their capabilities
5. Examine security models to understand how systems protect themselves
6. Learn about proxies and load balancing to understand traffic management
7. Study caching and CDNs to understand performance optimization at the edge
8. Deep dive into databases and data storage to understand data management at scale
9. Explore message queues and event streaming to understand asynchronous architectures
10. Study big data processing to understand analytics at massive scale
11. Learn containerization and DevOps to understand modern deployment and operations
12. Cap your study with the high-performance case study to see how all concepts converge
