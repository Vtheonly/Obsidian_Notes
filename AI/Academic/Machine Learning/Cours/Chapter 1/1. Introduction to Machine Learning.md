
# 1. Introduction to Machine Learning

## 1.1. Definition and Philosophy

**Machine Learning (ML)**—or *Apprentissage Automatique* in French—is a fundamental branch of **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**. Its primary goal is to provide computers with the ability to learn from data without being explicitly programmed for a specific task.

### The "Intelligence" Misconception
A crucial conceptual point to remember is the distinction between the hardware and the software:
*   **The Computer:** Is **not** intelligent on its own. It is simply a calculator.
*   **The Algorithm:** Is the component that exhibits "intelligence." It uses mathematical models to find patterns.

> [!INFO] Scientific Terminology
> You will often hear two terms used interchangeably:
> 1.  **Machine Learning:** Focuses on algorithms and prediction.
> 2.  **Statistical Learning:** Focuses on the statistical properties of the data.
>
> In this course, we treat them as part of the same domain: **Methods and algorithms for automated learning.**

## 1.2. The General Process
The fundamental flow of any machine learning process involves transforming inputs into outputs based on learned patterns.

### The Data Structure
Data is typically organized in a tabular format (Dataset):
*   **Vector $X$ (Inputs):** Also called **Descriptors**, **Features**, or **Explanatory Variables**. This is the information we know (e.g., Sugar level, Iron level in blood).
*   **Vector $Y$ (Outputs):** Also called **Labels**, **Targets**, or the **Correct Response**. This is what we want to predict (e.g., Disease Type).

---

## 1.3. The Role of the Expert
In the initial phase of creating a dataset (specifically for Supervised Learning), humans play a critical role.

1.  **Observation:** We collect raw data (e.g., medical symptoms).
2.  **Labeling:** An **Expert** (e.g., a doctor) analyzes the data and provides the correct diagnosis (the Output $Y$).
3.  **Database Creation:** This labeled data creates the **BDD** (Base de Données) or Dataset used to train the descriptor/algorithm.

> [!TIP] Exam Tip
> Never forget that the "Ground Truth" or the correct answers in a training set come from human experts initially. The machine tries to mimic this expert logic.
