# 5.3 Opening and Closing

Erosion and Dilation are powerful but they change the size of objects. **Opening** and **Closing** are compound operations that clean the image while attempting to preserve the original size of the objects.

## 1. Opening ($\circ$)
**Definition:** An Erosion followed by a Dilation.
$$ A \circ B = (A \ominus B) \oplus B $$

### Visual Logic: The "Ball Rolling" Analogy
Imagine the SE is a circular ball. The Opening of $A$ by $B$ is the set of all points reached by the ball as it rolls **inside** the object.
*   The ball cannot fit into narrow "isthmuses" (necks) or sharp outward corners.
*   **Effect:** It removes small bright spots (noise), breaks thin connections, and rounds off sharp "peaks" from the outside.
*   **Property:** It is *anti-extensive* (the result is always a subset of the original image).

## 2. Closing ($\bullet$)
**Definition:** A Dilation followed by an Erosion.
$$ A \bullet B = (A \oplus B) \ominus B $$

### Visual Logic
Imagine the ball rolling on the **outside** of the object.
*   The ball cannot fit into narrow "gulfs" or small holes inside the object.
*   **Effect:** It fills small black holes (pepper noise), joins narrow breaks, and smooths contours by filling in sharp inward "valleys."
*   **Property:** It is *extensive* (the original image is always a subset of the result).

## 3. Idempotency
A crucial property of both Opening and Closing is that they are **idempotent**.
$$ (A \circ B) \circ B = A \circ B $$
This means that applying the operation a second time changes nothing. Once the noise is removed or the hole is filled, it stays that way.

| Operation | Sequence | Main Goal |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Opening** | Erode $\to$ Dilate | Remove small objects / Smooth outward |
| **Closing** | Dilate $\to$ Erode | Fill small holes / Smooth inward |
