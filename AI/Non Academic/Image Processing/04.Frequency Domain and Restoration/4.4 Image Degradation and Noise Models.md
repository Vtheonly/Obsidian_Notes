# 4.4 Image Degradation and Noise Models

## 1. The General Model of Degradation
In a real system, the image we see ($g$) is the result of the original scene ($f$) being blurred by a degradation function ($h$) and corrupted by additive noise ($\eta$).
$$ g(x, y) = h(x, y) * f(x, y) + \eta(x, y) $$

## 2. Types of Noise ($\eta$)
Noise is categorized by its **Probability Density Function (PDF)**.

### A. Gaussian Noise
*   **Math:** Follows a Normal (Bell) distribution.
*   **Source:** Electronic circuit noise, sensor heat.
*   **Visual:** Fine grain over the whole image.

### B. Salt-and-Pepper (Impulse) Noise
*   **Math:** Pixels are replaced with 0 or 255 with a certain probability.
*   **Source:** Faulty memory, transmission errors.
*   **Visual:** Isolated white and black pixels.

### C. Speckle Noise
*   **Math:** Multiplicative noise. $g = f + (f \cdot \eta)$.
*   **Source:** Common in Laser, Radar (SAR), and Ultrasound imaging.
*   **Visual:** Grainy "sand" texture that is stronger in bright areas.

## 3. Restoration vs. Enhancement
*   **Enhancement:** Subjective. Making the image "look better" (e.g., Contrast Stretching). There is no "right" answer.
*   **Restoration:** Objective. Trying to undo a known mathematical degradation (e.g., De-blurring) to get back to the original truth.
