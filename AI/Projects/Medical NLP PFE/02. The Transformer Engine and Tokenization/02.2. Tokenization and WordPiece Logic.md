# 2.2. Tokenization and WordPiece Logic

Before BERT can do the math of Attention, it must turn human text into a list of numbers. This is a multi-step engineering process called **Tokenization**.

## 1. WordPiece Tokenization (Subword Intelligence)
Standard computers recognize words. But in medicine, we have words like **"Oculocutaneous."** If this word isn't in the AI's dictionary, it fails (Unknown Token Error `[UNK]`).

**Solution**: WordPiece breaks long words into smaller, meaningful chunks.
- **Word**: *"Oculocutaneous"*
- **Tokens**: `Oculo`, `##cut`, `##ane`, `##ous`
- **Logic**: Even if the AI has never seen the full word, it knows "Oculo" means "Eye" and "Cutane" means "Skin" because it has seen those pieces in other words. The double hashtags `##` signify that the token is a continuation of the previous word.

## 2. Infrastructure Tokens ([CLS] and [SEP])
BERT adds special markers to every sentence to help it understand structure:
- **[CLS]**: (Classification) This token is placed at index 0. Its final vector is used to represent the **entire sentence** meaning. **This is what your project uses for Cosine Similarity.**
- **[SEP]**: (Separator) Placed at the end of a sentence to tell the AI where to stop.

## 3. Padding, Truncation, and Masking
BERT requires all inputs to be the **same length** (usually 512). Since patient notes vary from 5 words to 500 words, we use:

*   **Padding**: We add "Zeros" (empty tokens) to make the short sentence as long as the long one.
*   **Truncation**: If a note is too long, we cut it off at the limit.
*   **The Attention Mask**: To prevent the AI from "paying attention" to padding:
    - **Mask = 1**: "This is a real word. Focus on it."
    - **Mask = 0**: "This is padding. Ignore it."
    - By setting the mask to 0, the math of the Attention Mechanism (the QKV products) becomes zero for those tokens.

---

## Tips for Presentation
- **The Dictionary Limit**: Mention that BERT has a fixed "Vocabulary" of about 30,000 tokens. WordPiece is the secret to how it communicates a million concepts with only 30,000 words.
- **The [CLS] Vector**: When asked where the sentence vector is stored, tell them: *"It is the final hidden state of the [CLS] token at index 0."*

```mermaid
graph LR
    A[Sentence] --> B[WordPiece Tokenizer]
    B --> C[Tokens + [CLS] + [SEP]]
    C --> D[Attention Mask: 1 1 1 0 0]
    D --> E[BERT Model]
```
