# 6.1 Feasibility: What a Solo Developer Can Build

## The Core Concept
The field of 3D Computer Vision bridges the gap between mathematically simple Euclidean equations and impossibly complex Deep Learning tensor processing requiring supercomputers. 

For a solo developer or junior engineer understanding basic Deep Learning, attempting to write an entire 2D $\rightarrow$ 3D pipeline from scratch is an impossible task. The secret to success in this domain is **Modular Pipeline Architecture**: identifying what you write yourself, what you call as a library, and what you exclusively download as pre-trained weights.

---

## 1. Implementable From Scratch (Do This Yourself)

These are the components an individual developer can and should write from scratch using NumPy, PyTorch, or Open3D. Writing these provides deep mathematical intuition.

*   **Pinhole Camera Projection:** Writing the loop that multiplies $p = K \cdot [R|t] \cdot P_{world}$. (See: [[1.3 Projection Matrices and Coordinate Transformations]]).
*   **Depth to Point Cloud Conversion:** Writing the Inverse projection equation to turn a 2D image into 3D $(X,Y,Z)$ tuples.
*   **Pipeline Orchestration (The "Glue"):** Parsing text files output by COLMAP, aligning scales, loading video frames using OpenCV, and dynamically injecting them into Open3D processors.
*   **Simple Triangulation (DLT):** Writing the math to intersect two 3D rays from two cameras. (See: [[2.3 Classical Point Cloud Generation]]).

## 2. Uses Pre-Built Algorithms (Do Not Write These)

These components rely on classical mathematics (Calculus and Linear Algebra), so they do not require "training." However, the math is so aggressively sensitive to floating-point noise and edge-cases that millions of hours have been spent by academics optimizing the C++ loops. 

Writing these yourself will result in code that is $1000\times$ too slow or mathematically unstable. Use the industry standards.

*   **Feature Detection (SIFT/SURF):** Call OpenCV.
*   **Structure from Motion / Pose Estimation:** Execute **COLMAP** via subprocess. Do not write your own Levenberg-Marquardt optimizer for Bundle Adjustment. (See: [[2.2 Bundle Adjustment and Optimization]]).
*   **Sparse Voxel Hashing / TSDF:** Call Open3D's `ScalableTSDFVolume`.
*   **Marching Cubes & Poisson Surfacing:** Call Open3D or MeshLab bindings.

## 3. Uses Pretrained Models (Download and Run)

These are advanced Artificial Intelligence Foundation Models. You cannot train them, but you can effortlessly execute them (inference).

*   **Monocular Depth Estimation:** Models like **Depth Anything V3** or **MiDaS**. You download the `.pth` weights, load them into PyTorch, and pass a single image tensor through to receive perfect contextual depth. (See: [[3.2 Neural Depth Estimation and Foundation Models]]).
*   **Semantic Segmentation:** Models like **Segment Anything (SAM)**. Used if you want to perfectly extract a single object (like a car) from the background before reconstructing it.

## 4. Requires Heavy Research-Level Work (Stay Away)

These are domains reserved for multi-million dollar robotics labs or massive VR companies (Meta/Google). 

*   **Training a Base Depth Estimation Model:** Requires $100$ Terabytes of physically perfectly aligned LiDAR and RGB datasets recorded rigidly on cars or robotic arms, plus weeks of A100 GPU compute. (See: [[3.3 Training Requirements for Depth Models]]).
*   **Writing a Custom CUDA Rasterizer:** The backend that makes 3D Gaussian Splatting run at 150 FPS. (See: [[5.2 3D Gaussian Splatting]]). 
*   **Developing a New SfM Optimizer:** Mathematically outperforming Ceres Solver or COLMAP's Bundle Adjustment.
