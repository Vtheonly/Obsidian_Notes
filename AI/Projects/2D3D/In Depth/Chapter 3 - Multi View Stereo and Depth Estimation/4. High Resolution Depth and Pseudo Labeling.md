# 4. High Resolution Depth and Pseudo Labeling

When generating the 100,000 custom building dataset to train your CasMVSNet, you must establish a Ground Truth depth map for each image. Because LiDAR scans for 100k images do not exist, we use a technique called **Pseudo Labeling**. 

We run state-of-the-art models (like DepthAnything V3 Large) over datasets like MegaDepth, capturing the outputs to serve as the "ground truth" for our custom network.

## The 16-Bit PNG Mandate
When extracting depth maps from Neural Networks, you must store the data properly. **Never save depth maps as JPEGs or 8-bit PNGs.**

*   An 8-bit image only holds 256 distinct values ($0$ to $255$). If your room is 10 meters deep, your depth resolution becomes blocked into massive 4-centimeter steps. Any angled floor will look like Minecraft stairs.
*   **16-bit PNGs** allow 65,535 distinct values.

**The Math in Your Code:**
```python
depth_16bit = (depth - depth.min()) / (depth.max() - depth.min()) * 65535.0
depth_16bit = depth_16bit.astype(np.uint16)
```
This logic maps the floating-point depth tensors directly into unsigned 16-bit integers, perfectly maintaining smooth gradients across vast architectural facades without data loss.
