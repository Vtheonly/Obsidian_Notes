# 2. Multi Scale Depth Fusion Research

## The Flaw in Standard TSDF
The standard TSDF works well if all cameras are the same distance from the object. But what if you have a wide-shot of a whole building (low resolution geometry), and a macro-shot of a carving on the door (high resolution geometry)?

If you throw them into the standard TSDF equation, the low-res data will mathematically "blur" and destroy the high-res details. 

## The Fuhrmann and Goesele Solution (2011)
The research paper you referenced solves this by introducing a **Hierarchical SDF**.

Instead of one voxel grid, they use an **Octree** (a tree data structure where each node has 8 children, allowing for grids of different sizes in the same space).

1.  **Scale Assignment:** When a depth map is loaded, the algorithm calculates its "scale" (how much physical 3D space one pixel covers). 
2.  **Level Sorting:** 
    *   High-res depth maps are integrated *only* into the finest level of the Octree.
    *   Low-res depth maps are integrated *only* into the coarse levels.
3.  **The Push-Pull Merge:** To extract the final mesh, the algorithm doesn't just average the layers. It mathematically interpolates them. 
    *   It takes the accumulated Numerator $(\sum W_i d_i)$ and Denominator $(\sum W_i)$ from the coarse layer.
    *   It uses **Trilinear Interpolation** to upsample these continuous functions to the fine layer.
    *   It adds them together *before* dividing.

By interpolating the accumulated values before the final division, the transition between "low detail" regions and "high detail" regions becomes perfectly mathematically continuous.
