# 3. Synthetic Data and Backward Warping

To verify stereopsis algorithms immediately without a 10GB dataset download, the architecture relies on a purely mathematical `SyntheticMVSDataset`.

## Simulating Physical Coherence
It does not just generate random noise. It simulates reality:
1.  Creates a base 2D image populated with colored geometry.
2.  Generates a continuous depth plane mathematically defined as a tilted surface with localized bumps.
3.  Calculates extrinsic matrices $[R|t]$ representing an orbiting camera.
4.  **Backward Warping:** It projects the 3D grid into the new camera frustums and uses PyTorch `F.grid_sample` to resample the original pixels into the new perspective.

This results in a perfectly mathematically coherent training batch where epipolar lines, depth, and pixel shifts align absolutely, allowing the network to train cost-volume matching on synthetic physics.
