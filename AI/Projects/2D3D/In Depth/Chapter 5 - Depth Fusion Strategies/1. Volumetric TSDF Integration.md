# 1. Volumetric TSDF Integration

Once we have dense depth maps from multiple angles, we cannot simply dump the raw points into a file. Due to sensor noise, the points from Camera 1 and Camera 2 will overlap into a thick, noisy crust. 

To solve this, we abandon points and merge **Continuous Space** using a **Truncated Signed Distance Function (TSDF)**.

## The Algorithm
1.  **Voxel Grid:** Divide the 3D bounding box of the scene into tiny 3D cubes (voxels).
2.  **Signed Distance ($D$):** For every voxel, calculate its distance to the nearest surface measured by the depth map.
    *   **Positive:** The voxel is floating in empty air between the camera and the wall.
    *   **Negative:** The voxel is buried inside the solid wall.
    *   **Zero:** The voxel sits exactly on the physical surface.
3.  **Truncation:** We only care about a narrow band (e.g., $\pm 4$ cm) around the surface. Distances outside this band are capped.

By defining the surface as the exact point where the values smoothly transition from Positive to Negative (the zero-crossing), we perfectly cancel out the random camera noise.
