# 3. Triangulation and Bundle Adjustment

## Triangulation
Once we know the camera poses (from the Essential Matrix) and the camera lenses (Intrinsic matrix), we can locate the 3D point.
*   Camera 1 shoots a mathematical ray through pixel $x_1$.
*   Camera 2 shoots a mathematical ray through pixel $x_2$.
*   Where these two rays intersect in 3D space is the definitive location of Point $X$.

Due to sensor noise and rounding errors, the rays rarely perfectly collide. Triangulation finds the 3D coordinate that represents the shortest mathematical distance between the two non-intersecting rays.

## Bundle Adjustment (The Optimizer)
If your camera rotation is wrong by even 0.5 degrees, shooting a ray 10 meters into the distance will result in massive errors, causing your reconstruction to look "blobby" or "ghosted."

Bundle Adjustment fixes this by optimizing everything simultaneously. 
1.  Take the estimated 3D point $X$.
2.  Project it back through the estimated Camera Pose and Intrinsic lens.
3.  Calculate the **Reprojection Error**: The distance (in pixels) between where the 3D point *projected* onto the sensor, and where the SIFT feature was *actually observed*.

Bundle Adjustment uses Non-Linear Least Squares (Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm) to slightly wiggle all 3D points and all Camera Poses until the sum of all Reprojection Errors is driven as close to zero as physically possible.
