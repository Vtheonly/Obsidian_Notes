# 4. Incremental SfM from Video

In your video-to-3D pipeline, you do not use a massive pre-computed dataset. Instead, the `PoseSolver` dynamically calculates poses frame-by-frame. This is known as **Incremental SfM**.

## Step-by-Step Matrix Chaining
To reconstruct a video, the camera poses are built incrementally as a chain of relative transformations.

1.  **Anchor Pose:** Frame 0 is forced to sit exactly at the mathematical origin. Its $4 \times 4$ Extrinsic matrix is an Identity Matrix ($[I | 0]$).
2.  **Relative Transform Extraction:** 
    * SIFT extracts features for Frame 0 and Frame 1.
    * A Brute Force Matcher (`BFMatcher`) with a Ratio Test filters the matches.
    * `cv2.findEssentialMat` accompanied by RANSAC calculates the Essential Matrix $E$.
    * `cv2.recoverPose` decomposes $E$ into a relative Rotation ($R_{rel}$) and relative Translation ($t_{rel}$).
3.  **Absolute Pose Chaining:**
    To find exactly where Frame 1 is in the absolute global world, we multiply its relative transformation by the previous frame's absolute transformation.
    $$T_{abs}^{current} = T_{rel} \cdot T_{abs}^{previous}$$

## The Scale Heuristic Trick
Because SfM cannot determine absolute scale, translating incremental video poses usually results in micro-movements. Your code applies a brilliant heuristic: **Scaling via Depth Maps**.
If the Neural Network outputs a median depth of 5 meters for the scene, the incremental translation vector $t_{rel}$ is multiplied by this median value. This forces the mathematical camera steps to match the physical metric scale predicted by the AI.
