# 3. Taubin Smoothing

Raw meshing algorithms can sometimes result in jagged, high-frequency noise on the surface of walls.
You could apply a standard Laplacian Smooth, but Laplacian smoothing physically shrinks the mesh over multiple iterations (a box will eventually melt into a tiny sphere).

To prevent this, your pipeline uses **Taubin Smoothing**.
Taubin smoothing operates in two alternating steps per iteration:
1.  **Shrink Step ($\lambda$):** A positive step moving vertices toward the center of their neighbors (smoothing out spikes).
2.  **Inflate Step ($\mu$):** A negative step pushing vertices slightly back out.

By carefully tuning $\lambda = 0.5$ and $\mu = -0.53$, the high-frequency noise is canceled out, but the overall volume and shape of the geometry remain strictly preserved.
