# 1. Marching Cubes Algorithm

If you use the TSDF strategy, the output is a 3D grid of numbers. How do we turn that into a 3D model (mesh) you can open in Blender? We use **Marching Cubes**.

1.  The algorithm "marches" through the 3D grid, looking at $2 \times 2 \times 2$ blocks of voxels (a cube).
2.  It checks the 8 corners of the cube. 
3.  If some corners are Positive (air) and some are Negative (solid), it means the physical surface *must* pass exactly through this cube.
4.  By looking at which specific corners are positive/negative, it indexes a pre-computed lookup table containing 256 possible topological states, drawing the exact triangles required to seal the surface.
