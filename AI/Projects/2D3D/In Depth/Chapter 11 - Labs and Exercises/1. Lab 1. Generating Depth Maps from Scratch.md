# 1. Lab 1. Simulating the Pipeline Inputs

**Objective:** Understand how extrinsics project a depth map into 3D space.

**Theory Applied:**
Given a pixel $(u, v)$ with depth $Z$, the 3D coordinate $(X, Y, Z)$ in camera space is derived using the Intrinsic Matrix $K$:

$$X = \frac{(u - c_x) \cdot Z}{f_x}$$
$$Y = \frac{(v - c_y) \cdot Z}{f_y}$$

To move this point into the global World Space, you multiply the resulting vector by the inverse of the Extrinsic matrix.

**Conceptual Python Workflow:**
1.  Load your Intrinsic matrix $K$ (from EXIF data or calibration).
2.  Load your Extrinsic matrix $T$ (from SfM).
3.  Load the Depth Image array.
4.  Use `open3d.geometry.PointCloud.create_from_depth_image(depth, K, T)`.
5.  Visualize the point cloud. If the points look like a room, your $T$ matrix is correct. If they look chaotic, your SfM step failed.
