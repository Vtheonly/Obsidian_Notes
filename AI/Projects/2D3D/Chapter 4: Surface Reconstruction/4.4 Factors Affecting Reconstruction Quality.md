# 4.4 Factors Affecting Reconstruction Quality

## The Core Concept
If a developer builds a mathematically perfect Volumetric TSDF backend and uses state-of-the-art Neural Depth estimation, the resulting 3D model can *still* look like garbage. 

The ultimate quality of a 3D reconstruction is almost entirely dictated by the physical constraints and quality of the visual data ingested before the math actually begins. 

"Garbage in, garbage out" governs 2D $\rightarrow$ 3D reconstruction ruthlessly.

---

## 1. Number of Views and Overlap

Volumetric fusion requires mathematical consensus. If only 1 camera sees the back of a statue, any mathematical depth error is written permanently into the mesh. 

*   **The Overlap Rule:** A single physical point in the world should ideally be observed by at least 3 to 5 separate cameras.
*   **Sweep Patterns:** When recording a video for reconstruction, "panning" the camera from one side to the other is terrible. You must physically move the camera body left and right (parallax translation) while focusing on the object. 
*   **Video Extraction Limits:** If you record a 60fps video and extract every single frame, the physical baseline distance between Frame 1 and Frame 2 is a fraction of a millimeter. The triangulation math ($x_2^T E x_1 = 0$) physically collapses when cameras are too close because the intersecting angle is $0.0001$ degrees. You must extract a frame every $0.5$ seconds to force a physically wide geometric baseline.

---

## 2. Camera Calibration Errors (Intrinsics)

If your camera's internal matrix ($K$) is wrong (See [[1.2 Intrinsic and Extrinsic Parameters]]), the mesh will permanently warp.

*   **Rolling Shutter:** If you take a fast video on a cheap smartphone, the top of the sensor scans before the bottom of the sensor. If the camera is turning, straight poles mathematically appear curved. The SfM triangulation will literally bend walls in 3D space to accommodate this.
*   **Auto-Focus / Auto-Zoom:** If the camera refocuses mid-video, its focal length ($f_x, f_y$) changes abruptly. COLMAP assumes intrinsic matrices are locked. A changing intrinsic ruins Bundle Adjustment. *Always lock focus on your phone before mapping.*

---

## 3. Lighting and Glare

Algorithms assume the physical world is Lambertian (diffuse matte surfaces).

*   **Shadows:** If you shoot a model outdoors and the sun moves over an hour, the shadow physically moves. SIFT will track the moving shadow instead of the ground, completely warping the ground floor coordinates. Neural Depth will estimate the deep black shadow as a physical hole in the floor. 
*   **Reflections / Overexposure:** Windows and shiny metallic car paints ruin classical MVS completely. Neural Depth Foundation models handle them moderately well by guessing context. Overexposed sky ruins clipping boundaries.

---

## 4. Feature Detection Failure 

Structure from motion requires unique visual markers to track Extrinsics.
*   **The Textureless Room:** If you scan a pristine modern minimalist apartment with blank white walls, polished grey floors, and zero clutter, SIFT will find $0$ matches. The pipeline will completely fail.
*   **The Forest Problem:** If you scan a forest, a million identical leaves blow slightly in the wind. SIFT matches Leaf A to Leaf B incorrectly. RANSAC cannot filter the noise. The math collapses. 

---

## 5. The "Building Problem" (Deep Learning Pitfalls)

While Classical models collapse on textureless objects, Deep Learning MVS networks struggle uniquely on large architectural structures:
*   **Repetitive Pattern Aliasing:** Buildings are covered in perfectly identical repeating windows and brickwork. The 3D cost volume easily gets mathematically confused regarding *which* specific window it is looking at, resulting in severe depth mismatches that manifest as "ghosting" or floating walls of noise in front of the actual building.
*   **Occlusion Confusion:** If a Lamppost or Tree obscures part of a building across $10$ out of $60$ images, neural networks struggle to decisively prune the occlusion. It often melts the tree geographically into the building's facade. 
*   **Loss of Mathematical Precision:** Artificial Intelligence is fundamentally a prediction engine making an incredibly smart "guess". It cannot perfectly model the strict physical reality of a light ray hitting a razor-sharp 90-degree corner. Deep learning structures inherently suffer from "surface noise" and "tearing" around sharp edges compared to physical traditional photogrammetry, which uses math to legally prove structural bounds.

### Best Practices for a Developer Capturing Data:
1.  Shoot video walking in a slow, wide arc around the subject.
2.  Lock the Exposure, lock the White Balance, and lock the Focus physically on your device before hitting record.
3.  Ensure the object is heavily textured. If an object is highly specular (like a shiny metal statue), professionally coat it lightly with dulling spray or baby powder if permitted to make it matte before scanning.
