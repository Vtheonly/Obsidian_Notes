# 3.3 Training Requirements for Depth Models

## The Core Concept
If an engineer chooses to push past using pre-trained "black box" Foundation Models (like Depth anything) and wants to architect, train, or heavily fine-tune their own Monocular Depth Estimation Neural Network, they cross a threshold from simple pipeline engineering into complex Deep Learning AI research.

This note aggressively breaks down exactly what is functionally required to execute the training phase of a state-of-the-art spatial transformer CNN.

---

## 1. What Exactly Is Being Trained?

In modern architectures (e.g., DPT - Dense Prediction Transformers, or U-Net CNN architectures), we are training a network that takes an $[H, W, 3]$ dimension RGB tensor matrix, forces it through an enormous series of convolutional down-samplings and transformer attention blocks to extract spatial "meaning", and mathematically decodes the tensor back out to an $[H, W, 1]$ dimension matrix where the single channel explicitly represents distance ($Z$).

### The Computational Weight
*   **Model Size:** 100M to 300M parameter weights.
*   **VRAM Limits:** Because depth estimation demands insanely high-resolution tensor propagation to prevent blocking artifacts on fine geometry (like chair legs or hair), sending batches of $1080p$ imagery through a 300M parameter network rapidly consumes VRAM. Training requires vast arrays of A100/H100 80GB GPUs.

---

## 2. Dataset Requirements (The Impossible Hurdle)

The defining constraint of training a Depth Neural Network isn't the calculus; it is physically acquiring the exact, indisputable "Ground Truth" 3D mathematics needed to tell the AI it guessed wrongly.

**You must possess paired datasets.** For every single regular digital 2D photograph you feed the AI, you must already have a flawless, millimeter-accurate 3D LiDAR scan of that exact physical environment from that exact framing angle.

### Typical Massive Datasets:
*   **NYU Depth V2:** Indoor scenes captured simultaneously with Microsoft Kinect RGB-D sensors.
*   **KITTI:** Outdoor driving sequences where RGB cameras are perfectly physically bolted next to Velodyne 3D laser-spinning LiDAR rigs.
*   **Synthetic Data (Hypersim/TartanAir):** Raw renders from Unreal Engine where the Z-buffer is mathematically extracted as absolute perfect Ground Truth.

> **Why a solo developer cannot build this:** Gathering 5 million photos of random environments with a perfectly physically bolted laser scanner is a logistical impossibility for individuals. This is the domain of massive robotics labs.

---

## 3. The Loss Functions (How the AI Learns)

If you have the data, how do you mathematically punish the network during Backpropagation? You do not simply subtract the AI's guess from the LiDAR scan. The geometric math is highly nuanced.

1.  **Scale-Invariant Loss (The Most Critical):**
    As detailed in [[3.2 Neural Depth Estimation and Foundation Models]], monocular networks cannot understand absolute physical scale metrics automatically.
    If the LiDAR says a building is exactly 10 meters away, and the AI guesses it is exactly 20 meters away *but gets the shape of the building perfectly correct*, we do not want to punish the AI massively. We want the AI to learn *shape and structure*, not absolute scale metrics.
    *Scale-Invariant Loss* mathematically normalizes both depth maps so that their mean variance is zero before subtracting them, effectively hiding the scale error and solely measuring the structural geometric shape error.
    
2.  **Gradient Matching (Edge Loss):**
    If the AI generates a weirdly soft, blurry gradient across the sharp 90-degree corner of a table, a basic subtraction loss might only see a $1cm$ numerical error and ignore it. 
    However, visually, a blurry corner ruins a 3D model immediately. We apply derivative calculus (Sobel filters) to heavily penalize the network specifically if it fails to generate sharp, sudden mathematical discontinuity cliffs at exactly the same $X, Y$ pixel where the LiDAR scan reported a sharp cliff.

3.  **Local Structural Similarity (SSIM):**
    Used to punish the AI for generating high-frequency static noise or rippling topological artifacts on completely flat topological structures (like blank walls).

---

## 4. Pseudo-Labeling and Domain-Specific Fine-Tuning

If paired LiDAR datasets are strictly impossible to acquire, researchers often increasingly rely on **pseudo-labeling**. This involves running a Foundation Model (like DepthAnything) on thousands of unannotated images (e.g., 20,000 pictures of building facades collected from street photography/drone views) to generate *predicted* depth maps. These predictions serve as the training "ground truth" for a new, specialized network.

### The Caveat of Pseudo-Labels
Because DepthAnything produces **relative depth**, calculating standard geometric triangulation metric loss is fundamentally impossible. A network trained purely on pseudo-labels will learn fantastic structural ordering (e.g., edges, planar regularity, orthogonal structures) but **not accurate metric depth for 3D reconstruction.** 

### The Realistic Training Strategy for MVS
If a solo developer wishes to train a Deep Learning Multi-View Stereo network (See: [[3.4 Deep Learning Multi-View Stereo (MVSNet)]]) specifically tailored for building architecture (which carries strong geometric priors like vertical walls and repeating windows), they must follow a hybrid curriculum strategy:

1.  **Stage 1 (Pre-training on Pseudo-Labels):** Train the feature extraction backbone on 20,000 domain-specific unposed images (buildings) annotated with DepthAnything relative labels. This teaches the network the deep semantic shape and priors of the specific domain target.
2.  **Stage 2 (Metric Alignment):** Fine-tune the network on an existing architectural rigorous multi-view stereo dataset that *does* boast true metric ground-truth, such as the **BlendedMVS Dataset**. This is critical—standard baseline datasets like DTU consist of tiny indoor objects under a spotlight; pre-training on BlendedMVS provides the large-scale, flat surface handling fundamentally necessary for rendering cityscapes and buildings without ghosting artifacts.

---

## Implementation Status 
* **Requires Training?** **Yes.** A fundamental, multi-week execution loop.
* **Solo Developer Feasibility:** **Requires Heavy Research-Level Work.** Unless you have a specific, tiny domain or use clever pseudo-labeling / targeted MVS fine-tuning (e.g., on BlendedMVS), attempting to train a generalized room neural depth estimator from scratch is completely non-viable for solo engineers. You lack the $100$ Terabytes of paired LiDAR datasets and the massive server racks required for VRAM-heavy spatial Tensor processing. Stick to downloading pre-trained `.pth` Foundation model weights or carefully fine-tuning existing architectures via domain-targeted datasets.
