# 6.3 Ablation Study and Inference Speed

To understand why TAMER works, we perform an **Ablation Study** (removing parts to see what happens) and evaluate the **Computational Trade-offs**.

##  Ablation Study (CROHME 2014)

| Configuration | Tree-Aware Module (TAM) | Tree Scoring | ExpRate |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Baseline (CoMER) | No | No | 58.38% |
| TAMER (Train-only) | Yes | No | 60.39% |
| **TAMER (Full)** | **Yes** | **Yes** | **61.23%** |

*   **Conclusion:** The addition of the TAM module during training boosts performance significantly (understanding structure). Adding the Tree Scoring Mechanism during inference pushes the performance even higher (enforcing structure).

##  Inference Speed and Computation

Adding structural awareness comes with a cost. Structural scoring requires calculating a dense relationship matrix for every candidate in the beam search.

| Metric | CoMER (Base) | TAMER (Full) |
| :--- | :---: | :---: |
| **Parameters** | 6.39M | 8.23M |
| **GFLOPs** | 18.81 | 19.79 |
| **Inference FPS** | 11.13 | 6.75 |

### The Speed/Accuracy Trade-off
While the full TAMER model is slower (6.75 FPS), it is much more accurate. For real-time applications, engineers can turn off the Tree Scoring during inference to achieve **9.45 FPS** while still benefiting from the structural training (60.39% ExpRate).

---
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Engineering Decision:** If your application requires high-speed mobile recognition, use TAMER (Train-only). If you are building a server-side "Math Solver" where accuracy is paramount, use TAMER (Full).
