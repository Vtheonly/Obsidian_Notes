# 6.1 Datasets and Evaluation Metrics

To prove TAMER's superiority, it was tested on standard industry benchmarks against previous State-of-the-Art (SOTA) models.

##  Datasets Used

### 1. CROHME (2014, 2016, 2019)
Originating from the Online Handwritten Mathematical Expressions Recognition Competitions.
*   **Size:** Relatively small (~8,800 training images).
*   **Format:** Converted from InkML stroke trajectories to grayscale bitmap images.
*   **Challenge:** Limited data makes it difficult for traditional sequence models to learn grammar without making bracket errors.

### 2. HME100K
A massive, large-scale collection of real-scene handwritten expressions.
*   **Size:** 74,502 training images and 24,607 testing images.
*   **Challenge:** Reflects "real world" noise, varying backgrounds, and extreme writing styles.

---

##  Evaluation Metrics

1.  **ExpRate (Expression Recognition Rate):** The strictest metric. The percentage of expressions recognized 100% correctly. A single missing character makes the whole expression wrong.
2.  **$\le 1$ error / $\le 2$ errors:** Tolerates minor token prediction errors, offering a "near-miss" metric.
3.  **Bracket Matching Accuracy:** A specific metric highlighting syntactical correctness, checking if `{` and `}` align perfectly.

 **Key Finding:** TAMER maintains a bracket matching accuracy of **over 92%** even on the most complex expressions, where baseline models often drop below 80%.

---
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Strictness over Speed:** While BLEU and WER are useful in NLP, in math, **ExpRate** is the only metric that matters for a valid, calculable output.
