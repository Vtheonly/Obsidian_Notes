# 3.3 Inference Scoring Mechanism

In research papers, this is often called the **Prediction Scoring Mechanism**. It's the moment of truth where the model uses its tree-awareness to decide which LaTeX string is the "correct" one.

##  The Beam Search Problem
During inference, the model uses **Beam Search** to explore multiple possible formulas. It might generate several candidates:
1.  `\sqrt { x } + 1` (Correct)
2.  `\sqrt { x + 1` (Incorrect structure)
3.  `v x + 1` (Incorrect character recognition)

A standard model would only look at the **Character Probability Scores**. But character scores can be misleading, especially with messy handwriting where a `\sqrt` might look like a `v`.

##  The TAM Scoring Process
TAMER adds a second "layer" of scoring. For every candidate formula generated by the beam search, the **Tree-Aware Module (TAM)** calculates its structural validity.

###  Steps of the Scoring Pipeline
1.  **Generate Candidates:** Beam search produces 5-10 different LaTeX strings.
2.  **Extract Features:** The decoder extracts the hidden features for each candidate.
3.  **Calculate Matrix:** TAM generates the Relationship Score Matrix $S$ for each candidate.
4.  **Compute Structural Score:** The model looks at the best relationship scores for each character.

###  The Mathematical Score Combination
The final score for a candidate $y$ is a **weighted sum** of the sequence score ($S_{seq}$) and the structure score ($S_{struct}$):

$$ S_{final}(y) = S_{seq}(y) + S_{struct}(y) $$

*   **Sequence Score ($S_{seq}$):** The sum of the log probabilities of each character predicted by the decoder.
*   **Structure Score ($S_{struct}$):** The sum of the scores from the Relationship Matrix $S$, representing how "rational" the parent-child connections are.

##  How it fixes the "Square Root" Error
Imagine the model is confused between $\sqrt{x}+1$ and $vx+1$.

*   **Sequence Decoder:** Might give both a similar score (around 0.8).
*   **Tree-Aware Module:** 
    *   For $\sqrt{x}+1$, it sees a strong connection between `x` and `\sqrt`. (Score: **HIGH**)
    *   For $vx+1$, it sees no logical connection between `v` and `x`. (Score: **LOW**)

By adding these scores, the model realizes that $\sqrt{x}+1$ is the structurally superior choice, and it picks the correct formula even if the visual handwriting was ambiguous.

##  The Results
According to the research paper, this scoring mechanism leads to a **3-5% increase** in overall recognition accuracy (ExpRate). More importantly, it nearly **eliminates syntactical errors** like:
*   Mismatched brackets.
*   Dangling subscripts with no base.
*   Orphaned fraction numerators.

---
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Key Concept: Rationality.** In TAMER, structural "correctness" is treated as a form of **Rationality**. A formula that follows the rules of math is more "rational" than one that doesn't, even if the characters look similar on paper.

> [!TIP]
> **Students Often Miss:** This scoring happens **during inference only**. During training, the model is pushed to minimize both losses simultaneously. At "test time", we use the scores to refine the final selection. This is similar to how a human might "re-read" a confusing sentence to see if it makes sense grammatically.
