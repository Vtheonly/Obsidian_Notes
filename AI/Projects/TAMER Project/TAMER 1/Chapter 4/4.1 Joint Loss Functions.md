# 4.1 Joint Loss Functions

To make TAMER powerful, the model must be trained to optimize both the sequence prediction and the tree structure simultaneously. This is achieved by combining their respective loss functions into a **Joint Loss Function**.

## 📐 The Total Joint Loss

The final loss function is a weighted summation, allowing end-to-end joint optimization:

$$ \mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_{seq} + \lambda \mathcal{L}_{struct} $$

*   **$\mathcal{L}_{seq}$:** Sequence Decoding Loss.
*   **$\mathcal{L}_{struct}$:** Tree Structure Prediction Loss.
*   **$\lambda$ (Lambda):** A balancing factor (typically set between 1.0 and 2.0) that regulates the importance of structural vs. character accuracy.

---

## 🧵 1. Sequence Decoding Loss ($\mathcal{L}_{seq}$)

The model attempts to predict the sequence $y_1, ..., y_T$. At each time step $t$, the decoder outputs feature vectors to calculate the probability of a character appearing. Standard **cross-entropy loss** is applied:

$$ \mathcal{L}_{seq} = - \sum_t \log (\text{softmax} (X_t W_o + b_o)) $$

Where $W_o$ and $b_o$ are the weights and biases of the output layer.

---

## 🌳 2. Tree Structure Prediction Loss ($\mathcal{L}_{struct}$)

The model uses the relationship score matrix $S$ (computed in the TAM) to estimate parent-child probabilities. Cross-entropy loss is applied here as well to penalize incorrect structural predictions compared to the ground-truth annotation tree.

$$ \mathcal{L}_{struct} = - \sum_t \log (\text{softmax}(S)) $$

### 🔄 Dual Optimization Logic
By backpropagating the Tree Loss into the shared Decoder hidden states, the Decoder is forced to organize its internal embeddings hierarchically. It mathematically prevents the model from generating random strings of variables without understanding their geometric relation.

---
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Key Insight:** Notice that both losses are computed at every time step $t$. This ensures that as the model learns to "read" a character, it simultaneously learns its "place" in the mathematical hierarchy.
