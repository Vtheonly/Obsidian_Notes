# 4.3 Two-Stage Pretraining and Curriculum Learning

Training a complex model like TAMER from scratch on a small dataset is nearly impossible. We use two advanced training strategies to stabilize convergence and achieve State-of-the-Art results.

## 🏗️ Two-Stage Pretraining

### Stage 1: Printed Math (Im2LaTeX)
We first train the model on millions of clean, printed math formulas (e.g., LaTeX rendered to images).
*   **Goal:** Teach the model the vocabulary, grammar, and basic spatial layouts.
*   **Benefit:** The model learns the "ideal" version of math before dealing with the messiness of handwriting.

### Stage 2: Fine-Tuning (CROHME)
We then use the pre-trained weights and train exclusively on the noisy, handwritten data.
*   **Goal:** Adapt the "perfect" knowledge to real-world variability.
*   **Caution:** We use a **Lower Learning Rate** during this stage to prevent "Catastrophic Forgetting" of the structural rules learned in Stage 1.

## 📈 Curriculum Learning

You don't teach a child Calculus before Algebra. Similarly, a neural network struggles if it sees deeply nested, complex formulas in the very first epoch.

**Curriculum Learning** sorts the training data by complexity (calculated by sequence length or tree depth).

1.  **Epoch 1-5:** The model only "sees" simple sequences (e.g., $a + b = c$).
2.  **Epoch 6-10:** Moderate complexity is introduced (subscripts, basic fractions).
3.  **Epoch 11+:** The model is exposed to extreme recursive nesting.

### Benefits
*   **Stability:** Prevents the loss from "exploding" early in training.
*   **Speed:** Sorting the `DataLoader` by length means batches have fewer `<pad>` tokens, reducing wasted computation.

---
| Strategy | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Two-Stage** | Solves the Data Scarcity problem. |
| **Curriculum** | Solves the Optimization stability problem. |

> [!TIP]
> **Domain Adaptation:** The transition from Stage 1 to Stage 2 is a classic example of **Transfer Learning**. The model transfers its "grammatical IQ" from printed math to help it understand handwritten math.
