## 6.2 Transformer Fundamentals
The Transformer architecture, introduced in the seminal paper "Attention Is All You Need," revolutionized sequence modeling by completely discarding recurrent layers (like LSTMs/GRUs) and relying **exclusively on the Attention Mechanism**.

*   **Parallel Processing:** Unlike RNNs, Transformers process *all* time steps of the input sequence simultaneously. This makes them highly efficient for GPU parallelism.
*   **Multi-Head Self-Attention:** This is the core. Instead of running attention once, "Multi-Head" attention runs several independent attention mechanisms in parallel. Each "head" learns to focus on different types of relationships:
    *   Head 1 might specialize in finding sudden pressure drops.
    *   Head 2 might track the diurnal temperature cycle.
    *   Head 3 might identify correlations between wind direction shifts and power output.
    The outputs of all heads are concatenated, providing a deeply nuanced understanding of the temporal dynamics.
*   **Positional Encoding:** Since Transformers process data in parallel (losing the inherent order), we must mathematically inject "time signatures" into the input data. This positional encoding tells the model which data point came first, second, etc.

*Why we used GRU over a pure Transformer in the final design:* While powerful, full Transformers often require massive datasets (millions of sequences) and significant computational resources to outperform well-tuned LSTMs/GRUs for specific time-series tasks. GRUs have an "inductive bias" for sequential data, making them faster and more data-efficient for our dataset size. Our final GRU model achieves excellent performance without the higher complexity of a full Transformer.
