## 5.4 Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) & Bidirectional Logic
### The GRU (Gated Recurrent Unit)
In your final model, you upgraded from LSTMs to GRUs. GRUs are a streamlined version of LSTMs. They combine the Forget and Input gates into a single "Update Gate" and merge the Cell State with the Hidden State. They achieve nearly identical accuracy to LSTMs but require significantly less memory and train much faster, which was crucial for your 560,000-row dataset.

### Bidirectional Processing
A standard GRU processes a sequence (e.g., 24 hours of wind data) from start to finish. However, sometimes understanding the *end* of a sequence can shed light on its beginning (e.g., knowing a storm *ended* in a specific way helps interpret its *onset*).

A **Bidirectional GRU** (Bi-GRU) contains two identical GRU networks:
1.  One processes the data forward in time (from Hour 0 to Hour 24).
2.  The other processes the data backward in time (from Hour 24 down to Hour 0).

Their outputs are concatenated. This gives the model a profound, symmetrical understanding of the temporal context, allowing it to interpret the "build-up" to a wind event and the "calm-down" afterward simultaneously.
