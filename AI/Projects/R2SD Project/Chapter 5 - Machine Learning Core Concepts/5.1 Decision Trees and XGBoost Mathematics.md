## 5.1 Decision Trees & XGBoost Mathematics
### XGBoost is NOT a Neural Network
**XGBoost (eXtreme Gradient Boosting)** does not use neurons, layers, or continuous calculus. It is a powerful, tree-based ensemble model built on discrete logical splits (if/then rules).

### How Decision Trees Handle Numbers
To predict power from square footage, a tree doesn't use a formula. It tests thousands of mathematical thresholds (e.g., `Wind_Speed < 8.5`). It splits the dataset into two groups based on this numeric condition and calculates the average power of the left group and the right group. It iteratively picks the split that best minimizes the prediction error.

### Gradient Boosting (The Ensemble Process)
A single decision tree is often weak and prone to errors. XGBoost builds hundreds or thousands of these trees sequentially, in a "boosting" manner:
1.  **Initial Prediction:** The first "tree" (often just a simple average or a single decision node) makes a base prediction (e.g., guesses $500$ kW).
2.  **Calculate Residuals:** The true power for a specific reading is $800$ kW. The **Residual (Error)** is $+300$ kW ($800 - 500$).
3.  **Train Next Tree on Residuals:** XGBoost then trains the *second* tree not to predict the power, but to predict the *error* of $+300$ kW. This tree might predict $+200$ kW.
4.  **Update Total Prediction:** The total prediction becomes the Initial Prediction + Tree 2's output ($500 + 200 = 700$ kW). The new error is $+100$ kW.
5.  **Iterative Refinement:** This process repeats. Each subsequent tree is built specifically to predict and correct the remaining errors of all the preceding trees.

XGBoost literally sums up the outputs of hundreds of small, error-correcting trees. It is the absolute undisputed king of structured, tabular data (spreadsheets) and is renowned for its speed and accuracy.
