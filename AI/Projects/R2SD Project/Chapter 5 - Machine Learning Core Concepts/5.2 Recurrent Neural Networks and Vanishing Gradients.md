## 5.2 Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) & Vanishing Gradients
While XGBoost is brilliant at finding static relationships (e.g., "High humidity + High heat = X efficiency"), it has **no inherent concept of time**. It processes every row as an isolated event, regardless of its position in a sequence.

### The RNN Approach
Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) were designed to process sequential data. They pass a "hidden state" (a vector representing memory) from one time step to the next. This allows the network to learn patterns across time.

### The Vanishing Gradient Problem
When doing backpropagation (the process of updating network weights based on error) across long sequences (like our 144 historical steps), the gradients (error signals) are repeatedly multiplied by small weights as they pass backward through time. By around step 10, the gradient shrinks to near-zero. This means the network completely "forgets" events that happened at the beginning of the sequence, making it unable to learn long-term dependencies.
