## 3.4 Wind Vectors and Dimensional Continuity
### The Compass Problem
Wind direction is provided in degrees ($0^\circ$ to $360^\circ$). Just like the time of day, $359^\circ$ (North-West) and $1^\circ$ (North-East) are physically the exact same direction, but mathematically they are $358$ units apart. This causes immense confusion for gradient descent optimization.

### Vector Decomposition
We decompose the wind direction into **Zonal (U)** and **Meridional (V)** vectors:
$$ U = WindSpeed \times \cos(WindDirection_{radians}) $$
$$ V = WindSpeed \times \sin(WindDirection_{radians}) $$

*   **U-Vector:** Represents the West-to-East force.
*   **V-Vector:** Represents the South-to-North force.

This ensures dimensional continuity. As the wind slowly shifts from $359^\circ$ to $1^\circ$, the $U$ and $V$ vectors change smoothly and continuously, allowing the Neural Network to process direction as a continuous physical force rather than a discrete jump.
