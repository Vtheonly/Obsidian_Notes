## 3.2 Solar Physics and the Clear Sky Index
### Atmospheric Opacity
Raw Global Horizontal Irradiance (GHI) measures sunlight hitting the panel. However, GHI naturally goes up and down as the sun moves across the sky (deterministic). 
To predict unpredictable weather, we need to separate the movement of the sun from the movement of clouds (stochastic).

We calculate the **Clear Sky Index ($k_t$)**:
$$ k_t = \frac{GHI_{measured}}{GHI_{clearsky}} $$

*   $GHI_{clearsky}$ is a theoretical calculation of how much sun *would* hit the panel if there were zero clouds (using models like Ineichen).
*   If $k_t \approx 1.0$, the sky is perfectly clear.
*   If $k_t < 0.3$, there is heavy cloud cover.

This feature gives the AI a pure, isolated metric for "Cloudiness," drastically improving accuracy by focusing on the unpredictable component of solar variability.
