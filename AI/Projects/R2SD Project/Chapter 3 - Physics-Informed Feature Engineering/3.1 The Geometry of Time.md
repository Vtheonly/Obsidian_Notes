## 3.1 The Geometry of Time (Cyclical Encoding)
### The Linear Time Problem
If we feed an AI the hour of the day as an integer ($0$ to $23$), the AI perceives time as a straight line. 
Mathematically, the distance between Hour 23 (11:00 PM) and Hour 0 (Midnight) is $23$ units. To the AI, these times look like extreme opposites. In reality, they are only 1 hour apart. This leads to abrupt, illogical jumps in the AI's understanding of daily cycles.

### Cyclical Encoding
We solve this by mapping time onto a circle using trigonometry:
$$ Hour_{sin} = \sin\left(2\pi \cdot \frac{Hour}{24}\right) $$
$$ Hour_{cos} = \cos\left(2\pi \cdot \frac{Hour}{24}\right) $$

By feeding the model both the Sine and Cosine coordinates, the AI mathematically understands that midnight seamlessly follows 11:59 PM. This drastically improves the AI's ability to learn consistent diurnal (daily) patterns in energy production and demand.
