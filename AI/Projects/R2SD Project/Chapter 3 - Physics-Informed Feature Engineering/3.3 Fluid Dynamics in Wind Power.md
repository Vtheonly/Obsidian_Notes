## 3.3 Fluid Dynamics in Wind Power (The Cubic Law & Air Density)
### The Kinetic Energy Formula (The Cubic Law)
In your initial runs, the wind model had a terrible $R^2$ score (~0.13). The AI was assuming that if wind speed doubles, power doubles (a linear relationship).

Physics dictates that the kinetic energy available in the wind follows the **Cubic Law**:
$$ Power \propto v^3 $$
If wind speed doubles, the power potential increases by a factor of 8 ($2^3$). 

We explicitly engineered this feature: `df['wind_cubed'] = df['wind_speed'] ** 3`. This provided the AI with a massive "cheat code," instantly skyrocketing the accuracy to over 0.85 by aligning the model with fundamental physics.

### Air Density ($\rho$)
Wind at 10 m/s in freezing winter generates significantly more power than wind at 10 m/s in a hot summer. This is because cold air is denser (heavier), meaning more physical air molecules are hitting the turbine blades per second.

We utilized the Ideal Gas Law to calculate Air Density:
$$ \rho = \frac{P}{R_{specific} \cdot T} $$
Where $P$ is pressure (in Pascals), $T$ is Temperature (in Kelvin), and $R_{specific}$ is the specific gas constant for dry air (approx. $287.058 \, J/(kg \cdot K)$). Feeding $\rho$ to the AI allowed it to differentiate between summer and winter wind forces.
