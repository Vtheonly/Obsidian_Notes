## 8.4 Real-World Edge Deployment & Streamlit
### Why Google Colab?
The environment utilized was Google Colab with an **NVIDIA T4 GPU**. Processing 3D tensors of 560,000 samples through Bidirectional GRUs requires massive matrix multiplication capabilities. Without GPU acceleration, this training would take days.

### The Path to Production (Streamlit & Edge)
To deploy this system to a real wind farm, it must move from batch processing to a real-time streaming architecture:

1.  **The Circular Buffer:** In production (e.g., inside a `app.py` Streamlit script), data arrives via SCADA Modbus every 10 minutes. The Streamlit session state maintains a "Circular Buffer" of the last 144 readings. As a new reading arrives, the oldest is dropped.
2.  **Inference Speed:** Because inference (predicting) does not require backpropagation, the lightweight frozen models can run on Edge Industrial computers (like a Raspberry Pi 5) installed directly at the turbine site.
3.  **The UI:** Streamlit loads the `.pkl` and `.h5` files into memory via `@st.cache_resource`. It passes the circular buffer into the models, extracts the Delta and Sigma, and renders the 48-hour forecast curve and 95% uncertainty band in under 50 milliseconds.
