## 8.1 Chronological Integrity & Data Leakage
In standard machine learning (like predicting house prices), you shuffle the dataset randomly before training using `train_test_split(shuffle=True)`. 
In Time-Series Forecasting, **Random Shuffling is a fatal error.**

If you shuffle the data, the model might use data from Wednesday to predict Tuesday. This is called **Data Leakage**. The model appears to have 99% accuracy in testing, but it fails instantly in the real world because it learned to "cheat" by looking into the future.

**The Implementation:** We utilize a strict **Chronological Split** (`shuffle=False`). The model is trained on the first $80\%$ of the timeline, and tested strictly on the final $20\%$ of the unseen future.
