## 8.2 Statistical Evaluation Metrics
We use specific metrics to interpret the model's performance:

1.  **MAE (Mean Absolute Error):** The average absolute difference between prediction and reality. Highly readable for humans (e.g., "The model is off by 2.28 kW on average").
2.  **MSE (Mean Squared Error):** The loss function used for training. By squaring the errors, it exponentially punishes "large" misses. This forces the model to avoid catastrophic grid failures.
3.  **RMSE (Root Mean Square Error):** Brings the MSE back into the original units (kW) while maintaining the penalty for large outliers.
4.  **$R^2$ (Coefficient of Determination):** Represents the percentage of variance in the physical world that the model successfully explains. 
    *   Our final wind model achieved an impressive $R^2 \approx 0.87$ considering the chaos of fluid dynamics. 
    *   Our solar model achieved $R^2 \approx 0.99$, indicating near-perfect physical correlation between irradiation and power.
