## 7.3 The Power Anchor & Differential Prediction
In the final evolution of the project, we introduced the **Power Anchor**.

### The Lag Problem
Time-series models often suffer from "lag." They simply output whatever the power was 10 minutes ago, shifted slightly to the right on a graph.

### The Differential Architecture
Instead of asking the Neural Network to output an absolute value (e.g., $1500$ kW), which is a massive numerical range to optimize, we shift the paradigm.

1.  We extract the **Anchor**: The exact, known power generation at the *last available historical minute* (Time $T=0$).
2.  We feed this Anchor directly to the very end of the network, bypassing the LSTM layers.
3.  We train the complex network to only predict the **Trajectory (Change)**.
4.  An `Add` layer physically adds the Anchor to the predicted Trajectory.

This grounds the prediction in reality. It forces the network to acknowledge exactly where the turbine is starting from, acting like a PID controller predicting the derivative $\Delta$.
