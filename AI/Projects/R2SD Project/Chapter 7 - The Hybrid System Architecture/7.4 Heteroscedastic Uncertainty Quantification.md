## 7.4 Heteroscedastic Uncertainty Quantification
Grid operators do not just need a point forecast. They need a confidence interval.

Most AI assumes **Homoscedasticity** (that the error/noise is constant). In reality, weather is **Heteroscedastic**: on a clear day, the forecast is highly certain. During a storm, the error margin is massive.

We modified the output head of the neural network to output **two** neurons:
1.  **$\mu$ (Mu):** The predicted delta/correction.
2.  **$\log(\sigma^2)$ (Log-Variance):** The network's estimation of its own uncertainty.

By using a custom **Negative Log-Likelihood Loss Function**, the model is penalized if it is "confidently wrong." It learns to increase the $\sigma$ value during chaotic weather, providing a dynamic 95% Confidence Interval band around the prediction. *(Note: We predict Log-Variance because standard deviation $\sigma$ must be positive. Predicting Log-Variance prevents the network from outputting a negative number and crashing the math).
