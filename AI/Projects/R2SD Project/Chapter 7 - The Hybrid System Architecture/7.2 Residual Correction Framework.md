## 7.2 Residual Correction Framework
Why combine them at all?

XGBoost is flawless at static relationships. If given the weather right now, it will guess the power perfectly... *if the weather is stable*. Because it has no memory, it fails during dynamic shifts (storm fronts).

In the **Residual Correction Framework**, the Neural Network **does not predict power**. It predicts the *Error* that XGBoost is about to make.

$$ \hat{Y}_{Final} = \hat{Y}_{XGBoost} + \Delta_{LSTM} $$

The LSTM looks at the past 6 hours, recognizes a pattern of falling pressure, realizes that XGBoost historically overestimates power in this scenario, and outputs a negative $\Delta$ correction.
